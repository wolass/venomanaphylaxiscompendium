Reviewer 1.

The authors have important observations on the concomittant use of beta-blockers and ACE inhibitors and mores severe anaphylaxis; possibly a profund biostatistical analysis of this aspect could help to find out whether this is just due to the premorbidity of this patients or an effect of these drugs.

This is a very important aspect, that we have also investigated previously. (https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25441633). We could partially investigate the influence of cardiologic drugs on the severity of anaphylaxis in a maouse model of anaphylaxis.

In our previous publication regarding risk factors in all types of anaphylaxis we have found that ACE-I and beta-blockers increased the risk of severe anaphylaxis - but also the effect of concomitant cardiologic conditions could not be isolated from the effects of drugs alone. Therefore a different experimental approach is needed to address this question.

Nevertheless, due to our deep interest in this topic and in order to partially answer the reviewers question - we have reanalyzed the effects of cardiologic drugs on the severity of anaphylaxis. Patients who were taking cardiologic drugs (i.e ASA, ACE-I, and beta-blockers) more often had severe anaphylaxis if they were taken in combination, when compared to monotherapy. This is in concordance with our previous observations. We have found evidence that combined use of beta blockers and ACE-I increased the severity of anaphylaxis in vitro, whereas these drugs had lower effect when taken alone and these data were confirmed in a mouse model.

Reviewer #3: MAJOR COMMENTS: This report focuses on a sub-population of the European Anaphylaxis Registry and attempts to define phenotypes of insect sting anaphylaxis (VIA). This is therefore a study of severe insect sting anaphylaxis (VIA). The exclusion of patients with mild-to-moderate VIA provides a selected dataset and limits the generalizability of the results. The authors should point out at many points in the introduction, methods, results, and discussion, that this is a study limited to severe VIA and does not represent the full spectrum of VIA. There is a lack of depth in the references.

Actually, this report considers mild, moderate and severe cases from grades II - IV according to Ring and Messmer, and we decided to to group the grades of 4 and 3 together as severe vs.moderate cases of grade II. We arrived at a distribution that was very suited vor statistical analysis as the two formed groups formed a nearly 50-50 split in VIA and non-VIA groups. Please inspect the figure 1D for details. Due to the reviewers comment we have revised the line 130 in the methods section as it was unclear that we also compared the severe cases (grades III+IV) to mild-to-moderate cases of grade II.
